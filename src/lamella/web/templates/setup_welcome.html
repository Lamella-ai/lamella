⚠️ Read this before continuing

Lamella is self-hosted financial software that sends transaction data to external AI services when you enable them. Understand the trade-offs before you set it up.

You run it. You secure it.

Self-hosted means you control where this is installed, who can reach it on the network, and how it’s backed up. Lamella ships with safe defaults (binds to 127.0.0.1, optional username/password auth) but cannot enforce them through your firewall or your hosting choices. If you expose it to the internet without auth or behind weak credentials and your data leaks, that’s your responsibility.

Recommended baseline: behind a tunnel that authenticates upstream (Cloudflare Access, Tailscale, VPN), with AUTH_USERNAME + AUTH_PASSWORD set as defense-in-depth, and /data + /ledger on a backup schedule.

Your ledger is sensitive financial data.

Beancount holds amounts, account names, card tails, vendor names, dates, narrations, and (with receipts enabled) document images. A compromise of the host filesystem exposes all of it. Treat /ledger like you’d treat the folder where your tax return PDFs live.

Enabling AI sends transaction data to a third party.

When you configure OpenRouter (or any AI provider) and turn on the classify cascade, every classified transaction is sent to that provider’s API. The data sent includes:

  • Transaction date, amount, currency
  • Payee / merchant string and narration
  • The account/card it was charged on (e.g. Liabilities:Chase:CC:4242)
  • Vector-similar historical transactions (more context for the same vendor)
  • Entity descriptions and account descriptions you’ve written
  • For receipt verification: the receipt image plus its OCR text

Choose your provider deliberately. Different upstream models on the same gateway have different data-handling terms. Look for zero data retention, no training on customer data, or enterprise-tier guarantees in the provider’s policy. If you wouldn’t hand a vendor your tax records on paper, don’t hand them your transaction stream through their API.

AI is opt-in. Lamella runs without an AI provider — classification falls back to rules-only. You can also turn AI off at any time from /settings.
