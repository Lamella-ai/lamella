Self-hosted means you secure it

Lamella runs on your hardware (or your VPS). You decide where it’s installed, who can reach it on the network, and how it’s backed up. The defaults are safe — bind to 127.0.0.1, optional username/password authentication — but Lamella cannot enforce them through your firewall, your reverse proxy, or your hosting provider’s settings.

If you expose the app to the internet without auth, or behind weak credentials, and the data leaks, that’s your responsibility. The Apache 2.0 license under which Lamella is distributed includes a no-warranty clause for exactly this reason.

Recommended baseline:

  • Behind a tunnel that authenticates upstream (Cloudflare Access, Tailscale, VPN), or on a private LAN.
  • AUTH_USERNAME + AUTH_PASSWORD set as defense-in-depth even when an upstream proxy authenticates.
  • /data and /ledger on a backup schedule.
  • OS-level firewall blocking inbound on the Lamella port from anywhere except the proxy / tunnel.

Your ledger is sensitive financial data

Beancount is a plain-text format. Files under /ledger contain:

  • Every transaction’s amount, date, payee, narration.
  • Account names you defined (often containing card tails: Liabilities:Chase:CC:4242).
  • Vendor identities and (with Paperless integration) document references.
  • Open and Close directives revealing the structure of your finances.

A compromise of the host filesystem exposes all of it. Treat that directory the way you’d treat the folder where your prior tax returns live.

AI features send transaction data to a third-party API

When you configure an OpenRouter API key (or any other AI provider this app supports) and turn on the classify cascade, every classified transaction is transmitted to that provider’s API over HTTPS. The data sent per classification includes:

  • Transaction date, amount, currency.
  • Payee / merchant string and narration.
  • The account / card it was charged on (full Beancount path).
  • Up to N vector-similar past transactions (so the model has historical context for the same vendor).
  • The entity description and account descriptions you’ve written for the relevant accounts.
  • For receipt verification: the receipt image (PDF / JPEG / PNG) plus its OCR text.

This is what makes the classifier work — context is what turns "Hardware Store, $48.12" into the correct expense account. But the trade-off is real: that context is, in aggregate, a detailed picture of your spending habits, business activity, and financial structure.

Choose your provider deliberately. AI gateways like OpenRouter route to many upstream models, each with different terms:

  • Some upstreams retain prompts for moderation/training; some do not.
  • Enterprise tiers usually offer zero data retention; community / free tiers usually do not.
  • The provider’s terms can change; what’s ZDR today may not be ZDR next month.

Look for explicit guarantees in the provider’s data-handling policy: "we do not train on customer data", "prompts are not retained beyond the request", "zero data retention", or an enterprise-tier agreement. If a provider does not state those terms in plain English, assume the safe-but-unfavorable interpretation.

AI is opt-in. Lamella runs without an AI provider configured — classification falls back to your rules and the system surfaces uncategorized placeholders where the rules can’t decide. You can disable AI at any time from /settings; existing classifications are not retroactively sent.

What is not sent

To anchor the previous section: with default settings, the following are not transmitted to any external service:

  • Your Beancount files themselves (only excerpts the classifier needs).
  • Authentication credentials, session cookies, or session tokens.
  • Backup archives.
  • The SQLite database (used as a disposable cache, not transmitted).
  • Any data when AI is disabled — the app is fully functional without external calls.

Paperless-ngx is a self-hosted document store that you operate; the receipts you upload there stay on your infrastructure. Lamella reads from the local Paperless API, never from a hosted Paperless service.

No warranty

Lamella is provided AS IS under the Apache License 2.0 — see the full license text for the no-warranty clause. Bugs in classification, in override-write, or in migration could in principle produce incorrect financial records. Because of this:

  • Treat the system as a tool that proposes work; the operator (you) reviews what lands.
  • Run bean-check after meaningful edits; the app does this automatically with baseline-subtraction tolerance.
  • Back up /ledger on a schedule. Lamella is fully reconstructable from the ledger files alone — those are the only canonical source of truth.
  • Do not rely on Lamella as the only record for tax filing. Cross-check against bank / credit-card statements before producing Schedule C / F.
