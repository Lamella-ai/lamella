Identity

Entity determines whose Schedule C / E this loan’s interest expense rolls up under. A loan owned by Personal deducts on Schedule A (mortgage interest); a loan owned by your business entity deducts on Schedule C / E.

Property secured — leave blank for unsecured debt (personal line, business term loan). Setting it ties the loan to a property, so the property detail page shows combined balance + headroom for HELOCs and second mortgages.

{% endcall %} {% call C.card(title='Terms') %}
{% endcall %} {% call L.pair_help() %}

Terms

Original principal + funded date are the only required fields here — they’re what the amortization model needs to compute principal/interest splits on every payment.

Term + APR are needed if you want the modeled monthly payment to match. Skip them and the system uses your monthly payment (est.) verbatim instead of computing it.

Payment due day drives the “next payment due” signal on the dashboard and in the calendar.

Revolving (HELOC / LOC)

Check this for any debt that doesn’t follow a fixed amortization schedule. Once revolving, the auto-classifier won’t try to split payments into principal/interest — you categorize each one by hand via record-payment.

Credit limit surfaces available headroom on the property panel. Informational only — no enforcement.

{% endcall %} {% call C.card(title='Escrow / tax / insurance (monthly)') %}
{% endcall %} {% call L.pair_help() %}

Escrow vs. tax vs. insurance

Only fill these in if your servicer collects them. Leave blank when you pay property tax + insurance directly.

The three fields are independent: escrow is the total amount the servicer pulls into the holding account; tax + insurance are the line-item monthly accruals the servicer pays out from that escrow. They typically don’t add up exactly — escrow includes a buffer.

Linked ledger accounts

Liability account is the negative-balance Beancount account that tracks principal owed. The model decrements this on every payment’s principal split.

Interest expense is where the interest portion of each payment lands (deductible on whatever schedule the entity rolls up to).

Escrow account sits on the Assets side — it’s your money the servicer is holding to pay tax + insurance on your behalf. Leave blank with a monthly escrow amount set, and we’ll auto-suggest a path like Assets:{{ loan.entity_slug or 'Personal' }}:{{ loan.institution or 'Bank' }}:{{ loan.slug }}:Escrow.

Close-out

Setting a payoff date without toggling Active automatically closes the loan. The amortization model stops projecting forward; the loan still appears in historical reports.

If the payoff is in the future, set it as a planned date — the dashboard will surface it at the right time.

SimpleFIN ID

If your bank exposes the loan via SimpleFIN, paste the SimpleFIN account id here. The classifier uses it to auto-route incoming statement balances to this loan’s liability account.
