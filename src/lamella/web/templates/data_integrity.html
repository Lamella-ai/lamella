The reboot scan walks every transaction in your ledger and stages it onto the review queue. Two failure modes have hit users:

  • Already-classified transactions duplicated. The scan stages every ledger txn — including ones already resolved — so they appeared as "pending" alongside the live ledger entry.
  • Reboot apply rewrites .bean files in place. It's a destructive operation. If anything goes wrong mid-write, the ``.pre-reboot-<timestamp>/`` backup dir is the only rollback. Custom metadata that wasn't preserved on stage will not come back on apply.

The flow ships off until those guarantees are rebuilt. If you understand the trade-off, set LAMELLA_REBOOT_INGEST_ENABLED=1 in the environment AFTER taking a manual backup of your ledger directory. The recovery card below lets you undo the "duplicates" bug if you already ran a scan.
